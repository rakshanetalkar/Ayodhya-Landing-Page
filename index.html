About Ayodhya

History

In the Atharva Veda, Ayodhya has been described as the city of God, and its prosperity has been compared to heaven. According to Ramayana, Ayodhya was founded by Manu. This city remained the capital of Suryavanshi kings for many centuries. Ayodhya is basically a city of temples. Even today, remains related to Hindu, Buddhist, Islam and Jain religions can be seen here. According to Jainism, five Tirthankaras including Adinath were born here. its importance its ancient It is rooted in history because this city has been the capital of the famous and glorious Kshatriyas (Suryavanshi) of India. Among the said Kshatriyas, Dasharathi is worshiped as Ramchandra Avatar. Earlier it was the capital of Kosala district. Ancient According to the mentions then its area was 96 square miles. Chinese traveler Hentsang came here in the seventh century. According to him, there were 20 Buddhist temples and 3000 monks lived here.

Traffic and Transportation

Air Route

The nearest airport to Ayodhya is in Lucknow which is about 140 km. Is at a distance of. This airport is connected to major cities of the country through various flights.

Railroad Track

Faizabad is the nearest railway station to Ayodhya. This railway station is situated on the Mughal Sarai-Lucknow line. It can be reached from almost all the cities of Uttar Pradesh and the country. There is also a direct train from here to Basti.

Road Route

Uttar Pradesh Road Transport Corporation buses run to Ayodhya from almost all major cities. Ayodhya is connected by national and state highways.
